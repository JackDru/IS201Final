Education

Brigham Young University - Marriott School of Business

As a sophomore in the finance program, I have built a strong academic foundation grounded in analytical thinking, financial principles, and strategic problem-solving. I currently hold a 3.74 GPA and have excelled in core coursework, including earning an A in FIN 201: Principles of Finance. This class reinforced my interest in valuation, capital budgeting, and financial decision-making, and continues to influence how I approach professional and academic projects.

My academic experience extends beyond the classroom through active involvement in several finance-focused organizations. I am a member of the Finance Society, the Investment Banking Association, the Private Equity & Venture Capital Association, and Brigham Capital. These groups have provided opportunities to learn from industry professionals, develop technical skills, and collaborate with peers who share my interest in corporate finance, investing, and capital markets.

One of the most influential courses I’ve taken has been Humanities 201, which explores the rise of Western civilization. This class broadened my perspective on culture, leadership, and the evolution of institutions—context that enriches how I think about today’s global financial systems. Together, my coursework and extracurricular involvement reflect a well-rounded educational experience that balances technical competency with strong critical-thinking and communication skills.
